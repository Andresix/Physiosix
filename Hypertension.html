Hypertension

Introduction

Hypertension is the consistent increase in blood pressure beyond the normal range.
Blood pressure is the force exerted by the circulating blood on the walls of the blood vessels.
In recording blood pressure readings, two measurements are noted, which are:
Systolic blood pressure which is the higher reading.
Diastolic blood pressure which is the lower reading.
Hypertension is diagnosed when measured on two separate days and the systolic blood pressure is greater than or equal to 130mmHg,
while that of diastolic is greater than or equal to 90mmHg.

Blood pressure classification

Blood pressure category Systolic (mmHg) Diastolic (mmHg)
Normal Less than 120 Less than 80
Elevated 120-129 less than 80
Hypertension (stage one) 130-139 80-89
Hypertension (stage two) 140 or higher 90 or higher
Hypertensive crisis Higher than 180 Higher than 120

Symptoms of Hypertension

Hypertension does not always show any obvious signs and symptoms, that is why it is vital to regularly check your
blood pressure to ensure that it is on the normal side.
These are some of the symptoms that could manifest with hypertension:

  • Headache
  • Anxiety
  • Irregular heart rhythms
  • vision changes
  • Nausea

Risk factors

  • Unhealthy diets such as consumption of excess salt, low fruits and vegetables
  • Sedentary life style
  • Smoking
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Obesity
  • Family history of hypertension
  • Advance in age
  • Kidney diseases
  • Diabetes

There are so many other risk factors associated to hypertension

Treatment of Hypertension

  • Medications such as beta blockers and diuretics
  • Life style modifications such as healthy diet, physical activity, reduction of alcohol intake and avoidance of smoking
  • Treating any underlying condition
  • Appropriate management of physical and mental stress

Complications of uncontrolled hypertension

Inappropriate management of hypertension can result into some fatal disease conditions
It includes the following:

  • Stroke
  • Heart failure
  • Kidney failure

Preventive measures against Hypertension

  • Consumption of healthy diet. Take more of friuts and vegetables. Reduce salt intake and consumption of processed foods.
  • Ensure you are physically active or participate in regular exercise routines
  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly
  • Reduce alcohol intake
  • Avoid smoking
