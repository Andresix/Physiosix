Diabetes Mellitus

Introduction

Diabetes is a metabolic disease condition characterised by elevated blood sugar level.
Overtime this causes damage to the heart, kidney, blood vessels, nerves and the eyes.
Insulin is the hormone responsible for the transportation of blood glucose into the appropriate body cells.
So Diabetes occur as a result of inadequate production of insulin or as a result of reduced sensitivity of the
body cells to insulin.

Types of Diabetes

  • Type 1 diabetes:
    It is also called juvenile diabetes or insulin-dependant diabetes. This condition occurs as a result of
    inadequate production of insulin by the pancreas, so this causes accumulation of glucose in the blood overtime.
  • Type 2 diabetes:
    This type of diabetes usually occur in adults. It occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin.

Measurement of blood glucose or sugar

Blood sugar level is measured using the glucometer.

Blood sugar classification Fasting blood sugar level (mg/dl) Post meal blood sugar level (mg/dl)
Normal 70-100 70-140
Pre-diabetes 101-125 141-200
Diabetes 125 and above 200 and above

Symptoms of diabetes

  • Increased hunger
  • Increased thirst
  • Increased rate of urination and amount of urine
  • Blurry vision
  • Fatigue
  • Unhealing wounds
  • Decreased sensation to body parts

Causes of Diabetes

  • The exact cause of type one diabetes is not known, but it is presumed that the immune system
    destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.
  • The cause of type 2 diabetes is a combination of genetic and lifestyle factors.
    Therefore having the family history of diabetes and being overweight or obese, increases the chances
    of having type 2 diabetes.
  • There is also what is called Gestational diabetes:
    This is diabetes that arises during pregnancy. It is caused by hormonal changes that occur in pregnancy.

Treatment of Diabetes

  • The main treatment for Type 1 diabetes is intake of insulin to cover for the insufficient insulin productio in the body.
  • For Type 2 diabetes, exercise and diet modification is adopted in the managemet of the condition.

Complications of uncontrolled diabetes

Inappropriate management of diabetes can result into some serious complications
It includes the following:

  • Heart diseases
  • Kidney problem
  • Damage to the blood vessesls
  • Damage to the nerves
  • Diabetic foot ulcer, which can lead to amputation
  • Vision loss

Prevention of diabetes

  • Regular exercise, atleast 150 minutes of aerobics exercise per weeek
  • Consume less fatty foods and carbohydrates
  • Consume more of fruits and vegetables
  • Reduce alcohol intake
