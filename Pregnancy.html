Care in Pregnancy

Introduction:

Getting good care before, during, and after your pregnancy is very important.
It can help your baby grow and develop and keep you both healthy.
It is the best way to be sure your little one gets a head start on a healthy life.
There are several factors to consider in order to maintain a healthy prenatal, perinatal,
and postnatal life. We will be lookig at some of these factors.

Key factors in pregnancy care:

  • Nutrition:
    If one is expecting to get pregnant soon or already pregnant, then it is advisable to go see a health practicioner for proper advice on dos and don'ts
    of pregnacncy. On nutrition a pregnant mother should take a lot of folic acids, other essential vitamins and minerals.
    These classes of food can be found mainly in fruits and vegetables.
  • Smoking and alcohol:
    A pregnant woman should avoid smoking both directly and indirectly from being around those that smoke.
    Alcohol too should be avoided. Smoking and alcohol consumption could affect the development of the child and
    result into some birth defects.
  • Antenatal care:
    Always visit the hospital regularly for your antenatal care. Report any complain or unusual feelings to your doctor for proper management.
    Never miss your antenatal care appointment.
  • Drug abuse:
    Avoid any form of drug abuse including over-the-counter drugs. Drug consumption without doctor's prescription
    could predispose your fetus to some developmental malformations and thereby resulting into birth defects.
  • Exercise:
    Being pregnant doesn't imply you adopt a sedentary life style. Being active during pregnancy makes you
    healthier and stronger for labour during delivery. It also makes recovery from side effects of pregnancy faster
    and reduces the chances of postnatal complications.
    It is safer to see your Physiotherapist for appropriate exercise prescription for your different stages of pregnancy.
  • Family and Love:
    Adequate love and care from family and loved ones is also paramount to the health of the pregnant mother and fetus.
    It is needed for both the physical and mental health of the pregnant mother.

Adequate care in pregnancy is highly paramount for the safety of the mother and the fetus.
